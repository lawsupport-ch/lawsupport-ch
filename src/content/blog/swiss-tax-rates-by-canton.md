Swiss tax rates by canton vary dramatically in 2026. The difference between the lowest-tax canton (Zug, at approximately 11.8% effective [corporate tax](/tax-accounting/corporate-tax-switzerland/)) and the most expensive major canton (Bern, at approximately 20.7%) is nearly nine full percentage points. On CHF 1,000,000 of taxable profit, that gap translates to roughly CHF 89,000 per year. Over ten years, that is CHF 890,000 — a material business decision, not a rounding error. This article provides a complete, data-driven reference for Swiss tax rates by canton covering corporate tax, individual income tax, wealth tax, and the key incentives that affect your effective rate.

---

## How Swiss Taxation Works: Federal, Cantonal, and Communal Layers

**Federal corporate income tax** is levied at a flat rate of approximately **8.83%** on taxable profit (after deducting cantonal tax paid). This rate is uniform across all 26 cantons.

**Cantonal corporate income tax** is set independently by each of Switzerland's 26 cantons. Cantons have wide constitutional latitude to set their own rates, deductions, and special regimes. This is the primary variable that drives differences between cantons.

**Communal (municipal) corporate income tax** is a percentage — often expressed as a multiplier of the cantonal tax — levied by the specific commune where your company has its registered seat. Different communes within the same canton can have meaningfully different effective rates.

When this article refers to "effective corporate tax rate," it means the **combined federal + cantonal + communal rate** using the commune that produces the most commonly referenced rate for that canton.

## Why Zug Has the Lowest Corporate Tax Rate in Switzerland

Zug's tax advantage is not accidental. The canton has deliberately cultivated a low-tax, business-friendly environment for decades, building a cluster of international companies, commodity traders, crypto and fintech firms, asset managers, and [holding companies](/company-formation/holding-company-switzerland/).

**Cantonal tax rate:** Zug sets one of Switzerland's lowest cantonal rates on corporate income.

**Communal multipliers:** The city of Zug and the commune of Baar both apply very low communal multipliers. Most communes in Switzerland apply multipliers well above 100%; Zug's leading communes are among the lowest in the country.

**Federal rate is fixed:** The federal rate of approximately 8.83% is identical for every Swiss company. Competition between cantons occurs entirely in the cantonal and communal layers. Zug has minimised both.

The result: a company in the city of Zug pays roughly **11.8% combined**. The same company in Zurich pays approximately **19.7%** — and in Bern, approximately **20.7%**.

## Wealth Tax by Canton

Switzerland is one of the very few countries that levies an annual **wealth tax** on individuals' net assets. This tax is purely cantonal and communal — there is no federal wealth tax.

**General pattern:**
- **Zug:** Wealth tax rates are among Switzerland's lowest
- **Zurich, Bern, Geneva:** Materially higher wealth tax rates, adding thousands to tens of thousands of CHF annually for high-net-worth individuals
- **Schwyz, Nidwalden, Obwalden:** Very competitive wealth tax rates

For business owners holding significant shareholdings, the wealth tax on the imputed value of those shares can be material. Structuring through a [holding company in Switzerland](/company-formation/holding-company-switzerland/) and selecting the right canton for personal residence is an integrated planning exercise.

## Capital Gains on Shareholdings: Participation Exemption

**For corporations:** Switzerland offers a **participation exemption** (Beteiligungsabzug) that applies at both federal and cantonal level. A Swiss company holding at least **10% of the share capital** of another company, or shares with a fair market value of at least **CHF 1,000,000**, can claim a tax reduction on dividends and capital gains from those holdings — effectively reducing the tax to near zero.

**For individuals:** Switzerland does **not levy a federal or cantonal capital gains tax on private investment income** for private investors. Gains from selling publicly listed securities are tax-free. The exception is **professional traders**, whose gains may be reclassified as taxable income.

## IP Box / Patent Box: Up to 90% Relief on Qualifying IP Income

Following the 2020 STAF reform, **all 26 cantons offer a Patent Box regime** for qualifying intellectual property income. The canton-level Patent Box provides a tax reduction of up to **90% on net income from qualifying IP**. For a detailed breakdown of the Swiss Patent Box and [Swiss tax incentives](/tax-accounting/swiss-tax-incentives/), see our dedicated guide.

## R&D Super-Deduction

Cantons can allow an **R&D super-deduction** of up to 150% of qualifying research and development expenditure. A company spending CHF 1,000,000 on qualifying Swiss R&D can deduct CHF 1,500,000 from cantonal taxable income. Cantons like Zug, combined with the Patent Box and R&D super-deduction, offer genuinely competitive structures for IP holding and development companies.

## Anti-Avoidance Considerations: Substance Requirements

Choosing Zug for its tax rate does not automatically deliver the tax outcome. Swiss domestic law and OECD BEPS standards require **genuine economic substance** in the canton of registration.

- A **management company or operating company** needs local management, real office premises, local staff with genuine decision-making authority, and demonstrable business activities
- A **holding company** generally requires a registered office, at least one board member with Swiss residence, and genuine oversight of subsidiaries
- An **IP company** must satisfy the nexus requirement with genuine connection to R&D expenditures

Simply inserting a letterbox company in Zug while all management and operations occur elsewhere will not provide the anticipated tax treatment and creates risk of recharacterisation. The correct approach is to integrate genuine business substance — which, in Zug, is entirely achievable given the existing business cluster.

## Commune Within Canton: City of Zug vs. Baar

Within Zug canton, the **city of Zug** itself offers one of the lowest communal tax multipliers in the country. The commune of **Baar** is similarly competitive and hosts a large number of multinational companies and commodity trading firms. Other communes within Zug — including Cham, Risch, Steinhausen, and Huenenberg — are also below the Swiss average.

## Choosing the Right Canton: Practical Guidance

1. **Zug** is the clear choice for most internationally oriented businesses — commodity trading, technology, IP holding, asset management, crypto/blockchain, and family offices
2. **Nidwalden and Lucerne** are strong alternatives if operational reasons favour central Switzerland outside Zug
3. **Geneva and Vaud** are relevant for businesses needing access to the Geneva international ecosystem or French-language operations
4. **Zurich** should be chosen for operational, talent, and infrastructure reasons — not for tax efficiency
5. **Bern** rarely features in corporate location decisions driven by tax

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the lowest corporate tax canton in Switzerland in 2026?**

Zug remains the lowest-tax canton for corporate income tax in 2026, with an effective combined rate of approximately 11.8% in the city of Zug. Nidwalden (~12.0%) and Lucerne (~12.3%) are the next most competitive cantons. The federal flat rate of approximately 8.83% applies identically in every canton.

**Is it legal to incorporate in Zug purely for tax purposes?**

Yes — Switzerland's cantonal system specifically allows companies to choose their canton of domicile, including for tax reasons. However, a company in Zug must have genuine economic substance (real management activity, actual office presence, local decision-making) to validly claim Zug's rates. A letterbox arrangement can be challenged.

**How does the Swiss Patent Box work, and does it apply in all cantons?**

All 26 cantons are required to offer a Patent Box under the 2020 STAF reform, providing up to 90% reduction on net income from qualifying IP at the cantonal and communal level. The federal rate is not reduced. Companies must satisfy the OECD nexus requirement — the IP must have a genuine connection to Swiss R&D activities.

**How different is the personal income tax rate between Zug and Zurich?**

The top marginal rate in Zug is approximately 22.2%, versus approximately 39.7% in Zurich — a gap of roughly 17.5 percentage points. For someone with CHF 500,000 annual taxable income, this difference amounts to approximately CHF 87,500 per year in additional tax in Zurich.

**What is the federal corporate tax rate in Switzerland?**

The federal corporate income tax is approximately 8.83% on taxable profit, uniform across all 26 cantons. The variation between cantons is driven entirely by cantonal and communal rates.

**How much can I save by incorporating in Zug instead of Zurich?**

A company with CHF 1,000,000 in annual profit saves approximately CHF 79,000 per year in Zug versus Zurich. Over 10 years, that amounts to CHF 790,000. For companies earning CHF 5,000,000+, the annual saving exceeds CHF 395,000.

**Does Switzerland have a wealth tax?**

Yes, at the cantonal and communal level only. Zug has among the lowest rates, while Geneva, Bern, and Zurich impose materially higher rates.

**Are capital gains on shares taxed in Switzerland?**

For private individuals, gains on listed securities are tax-free. For corporations, the participation exemption reduces the tax on qualifying holdings to near zero. Professional traders' gains may be reclassified as taxable income.

**What is the participation exemption in Switzerland?**

A Swiss company holding at least 10% of another company's shares, or shares worth CHF 1,000,000+, can claim a proportional tax reduction on dividends and capital gains, effectively reducing the tax to near zero.

**Can communal tax rates vary within the same canton?**

Yes. Communal tax is expressed as a multiplier of the cantonal tax, and different communes set different multipliers. In Zug, the city of Zug and Baar have the lowest multipliers; other communes are slightly higher but still below the Swiss average.

**How did the 2020 STAF reform change cantonal tax rates?**

STAF abolished old ring-fencing regimes and introduced OECD-compliant incentives. Several cantons, notably Geneva (~14%) and Vaud (~13.8%), significantly reduced their headline rates post-reform to remain competitive.

**What substance requirements apply when choosing a low-tax canton?**

Companies must have genuine economic substance in their registered canton. Operating companies need local management, premises, and staff. Holding companies need a registered office, at least one Swiss-resident board member, and genuine subsidiary oversight.
